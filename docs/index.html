Evaluation of the Fréchet Radar Distance for the Quality Assessment of Synthetic Radar Data

Nicolas Zander*
OTH Regensburg - Aumovio
2026
PDF Code

Abstract

The evaluation of synthetic radar data represents a central challenge in the field of generative models, as established quality metrics such as the Fréchet Inception Distance (FID) rely on domain-specific feature extractors and cannot be directly transferred to radar data. In this thesis, the Fréchet Radar Distance (FRD) is introduced and systematically evaluated. The FRD is a distance measure inspired by the FID that employs random projections as a feature extractor and therefore does not depend on domain-specific pretrained models. To validate the FRD, a controlled test environment with parameterized pseudo-radar generators is employed, enabling targeted variation of statistical properties in the generated radar point clouds. An approximated log-likelihood serves as a reference metric, quantifying the true similarity between the underlying data distributions. The experimental results demonstrate that, within these controlled scenarios, the FRD provides consistent, reproducible, and theoretically expected assessments and correlates with the approximated log-likelihood. Hence, the FRD is suitable for robustly capturing structural similarities between radar data distributions. Building upon this validation, the FRD is applied to evaluate U-Net-based diffusion models for the generation of synthetic radar data. Radar data are characterized by sparse, noisy, and physically governed structures that fundamentally differ from image data. The results indicate that, under idealized training conditions, diffusion models are capable of reproducing these characteristics. Additionally, the influence of model capacity is analyzed, showing that beyond a certain model size, no significant improvement in quality can be observed.
